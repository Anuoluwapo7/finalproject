Influx in Refugee Admittance Over Ten Years

From 2006 to 2015, America encountered a wave of immigrants of refugees due to wars, religious persecution, famine happening in other countries. These data visualizations compare the refugee statuses of different countries and provides answers to why certain countries had higher trends than others.

A prevalent trend in this data is the continents Africa and Asia having the most refugees. This is because of two main things. Africa is made up of a collective of third-world countries that consistently deal with infrastructure issues and curropt governments. Asia, on the other hand, is in competition with Africa mainly because of three countries, Burma, also known as Mayanmar, Iraq, and Bhutan. These three countries were consistently dealing with civil wars mainly over government, religion and freedom.
